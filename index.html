Introduction to Finance

Finance is the study of how money is earned, managed, saved, invested, and spent by individuals, businesses, and governments.

Finance is part of daily life, but many people struggle due to lack of basic knowledge and language barriers. MYMONEY helps users understand finance in a simple and relatable way.

Finance is the management of money, including saving, spending, investing, and borrowing, to achieve financial stability and growth.

  • Saving: Keeping money aside for future use.
  • Budgeting: Planning income and expenses to avoid overspending.
  • Banking: Using banks to deposit, withdraw, transfer money (UPI, ATM, Net Banking).
  • Digital Payments: UPI, debit cards, credit cards, wallets (Google Pay, PhonePe).
  • Investment: Using money to earn more money over time.
  • Risk: Chance of losing money in an investment.
  • Loan: Borrowed money that must be repaid with interest.

Why Finance is Important?

  • Helps manage money wisely
  • Prevents debt problems
  • Encourages savings and investment
  • Builds financial independence
  • Helps in long-term goals (education, home, business)

UPI & Digital Payment Safety

UPI is a digital payment system developed by NPCI allowing instant transfers using apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm.

Digital Payment Safety

  • Never share OTP or PIN
  • Use strong passwords
  • Avoid clicking unknown links
  • Use trusted apps only
  • Check transaction details
  • Use screen lock
  • Keep your phone safe
  • Do not allow unknown app permissions
  • Do not trust urgent payment calls
  • Enable 2-Factor Authentication

Relatable Platforms

  • Google Pay
  • PhonePe
  • Paytm
  • BHIM UPI

Important Q&A

Q: Approve unknown payment request?
A: No, it may be fraud.

Q: Does customer care ask for PIN?
A: Never.

Q: Phone lost?
A: Disable UPI immediately.

Saving & Budgeting Basics

Saving means setting aside money for future needs. Budgeting plans income and expenses.

Importance of Saving

  • Financial security
  • Emergency handling
  • Discipline
  • Long-term goals

Importance of Budgeting

  • Controls spending
  • Tracks expenses
  • Encourages saving
  • Prevents debt

50–30–20 Rule

  • 50% Needs
  • 30% Wants
  • 20% Savings

Steps to Create Budget

  1. Calculate income
  2. List fixed expenses
  3. List variable expenses
  4. Set saving goals
  5. Track spending

Important Q&A

Q: Why should students save?
A: For emergencies.

Q: Is budgeting useful for small income?
A: Yes.

Q: Budget review?
A: Monthly.
