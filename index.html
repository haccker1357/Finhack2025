Introduction to Finance

Finance is the management of money including earning, saving, spending, investing, and borrowing. Understanding finance helps individuals make informed decisions, avoid debt traps, and plan for the future.

  • Saving: Setting aside money for future needs
  • Budgeting: Planning income and expenses
  • Banking: Deposits, withdrawals, transfers
  • Digital Payments: UPI, cards, wallets
  • Loans: Borrowed money repaid with interest

UPI & Digital Payment Safety

UPI allows instant money transfer between bank accounts using apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm and BHIM.

Safety Tips

  • Never share UPI PIN or OTP
  • Do not click unknown links
  • Verify receiver name before payment
  • Use only trusted apps
  • Enable screen lock and app lock

Important Q&A

Q: Should you approve a request from unknown number?
A: No, it may be fraud.

Q: Do banks ask for UPI PIN?
A: Never.

Q: What if money is debited wrongly?
A: Contact bank immediately through official app.

Saving & Budgeting Basics

Saving is setting aside money for future needs. Budgeting helps track income and expenses to avoid overspending.

50–30–20 Rule

  • 50% Needs
  • 30% Wants
  • 20% Savings

Q&A

Q: Is budgeting useful for students?
A: Yes, it builds financial discipline early.

Q: Why track expenses?
A: To control unnecessary spending.

Loan & Interest Basics

A loan is borrowed money repaid with interest over time.

Types of Interest

Simple Interest: Calculated only on principal.

Compound Interest: Calculated on principal + interest.

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment)

EMI is a fixed monthly payment made to repay a loan. It includes both principal and interest components.

Loan Q&A

Q: What happens if EMI is missed?
A: Penalty, low credit score.

Q: Is interest always bad?
A: No, but high-interest loans are risky.

Banking Basics

Banks safely store money and provide financial services such as deposits, withdrawals, loans and transfers.

Types of Accounts

  • Savings Account
  • Current Account
  • Fixed Deposit

Banking Services

  • ATM withdrawals
  • Debit cards
  • Online banking
  • Cheque facility

Banking Q&A

Q: Is money safe in banks?
A: Yes, banks are regulated by RBI.

Q: Can students open accounts?
A: Yes, zero-balance accounts are available.

Tax Basics

Tax is money paid to the government for public services like roads, education and healthcare.

Types of Tax

  • Direct Tax (Income Tax)
  • Indirect Tax (GST)

Why Tax is Important

  • Funds national development
  • Legal responsibility
  • Avoid penalties

Tax Q&A

Q: Do students pay tax?
A: Only if income exceeds exemption limit.

Q: What is Income Tax Return?
A: A form declaring income and tax paid.
