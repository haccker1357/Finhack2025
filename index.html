Introduction to Finance

Finance is the study of how money is earned, managed, saved, invested, and spent by individuals, businesses, and governments. Finance plays an important role in everyday life, from managing personal expenses to running large organizations and governments.

Finance is part of daily life, but many people struggle due to lack of basic knowledge and language barriers. MYMONEY helps users understand finance in a simple and relatable way. Understanding the basics of finance helps individuals make informed financial decisions, avoid unnecessary debt, and build a secure financial future.

Finance is the management of money, including saving, spending, investing, and borrowing, to achieve financial stability and growth.

Saving – Keeping money aside for future use.

Budgeting – Planning income and expenses to avoid overspending.

Banking – Using banks to deposit, withdraw, transfer money (UPI, ATM, Net Banking).

Digital Payments – UPI, debit cards, credit cards, wallets (Google Pay, PhonePe).

Investment – Using money to earn more money over time.

Risk – Chance of losing money in an investment.

Loan – Borrowed money that must be repaid with interest.

Why Finance is Important?

  • Helps manage money wisely
  • Prevents debt problems
  • Encourages savings and investment
  • Builds financial independence
  • Helps in long-term goals (education, home, business)

UPI & DIGITAL PAYMENT SAFETY

Explanation

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) is a digital payment system developed by NPCI that allows instant money transfer between bank accounts using mobile apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm.

To ensure secure and smooth transactions, users must follow basic safety practices and use digital payment systems responsibly.

UPI works 24×7 and requires a UPI ID and secure authentication like a PIN.

Digital Payment Safety (Important)

  • Never share OTP or PIN.
  • Use strong passwords.
  • Avoid clicking unknown links.
  • Use trusted apps only.
  • Check transaction details before paying.
  • Use a strong screen lock (PIN, fingerprint, or face lock).
  • Always keep your mobile phone with you.
  • Do not allow unknown apps access to SMS or phone permissions.
  • Don’t trust urgent calls demanding payment or action.
  • Enable 2-Factor Authentication & strong passwords.

Relatable Platforms

  • Google Pay
  • PhonePe
  • Paytm
  • BHIM UPI

Important Q&A

Q: Should you approve a payment request from an unknown number?
Answer: No. It may be a fraud.

Q: Does customer care ask for UPI PIN?
Answer: No. Never share it.

Q: Do banks ask for UPI PIN?
Answer: Never share your UPI PIN. Banks never ask for OTP or PIN.

Q: Why is digital payment safety important?
Answer: Digital payment safety protects user data, prevents unauthorized access, and ensures secure and reliable transactions.

Q: What should be done if a phone is lost?
Answer: UPI access should be immediately disabled through the bank or app, and the phone should be secured.

Advantages of Safe Digital Payments

  • Fast and cashless transactions.
  • Reduced dependency on physical money.
  • Better financial tracking.
  • Increased transparency.

SAVING & BUDGETING BASICS

Saving means setting aside a portion of your income for future use instead of spending it immediately. Saving helps meet future needs, handle emergencies, and achieve financial goals.

Budgeting is the process of planning how to spend and save money based on income and expenses. It helps ensure that expenses do not exceed income. Budgeting helps track expenses and control unnecessary spending.

Importance of Saving

  • Provides financial security.
  • Helps manage emergencies.
  • Builds discipline in money management.
  • Supports long-term goals like education, home, or business.

Importance of Budgeting

  • Controls unnecessary spending.
  • Helps track income and expenses.
  • Encourages regular saving.
  • Prevents debt.

50–30–20 Rule

  • 50% → Needs (food, transport, bills)
  • 30% → Wants (entertainment, shopping)
  • 20% → Savings

Relatable Examples

  • Pocket money
  • Monthly stipend
  • First salary

Steps to Create a Simple Budget

  1. Calculate monthly income
  2. List fixed expenses (fees, transport)
  3. List variable expenses (food, leisure)
  4. Set saving goals
  5. Track spending regularly

Important Q&A

Q: Why should students save money?
Answer: For emergencies and future needs.

Q: Is budgeting useful for small income?
Answer: Yes, budgeting works for all income levels.

Q: How often should a budget be reviewed?
Answer: A budget should be reviewed monthly.

Q: What is the difference between needs and wants?
Answer: Needs are essential expenses like food and education, while wants are non-essential expenses like entertainment and luxury items.

Q: Why should expenses be recorded?
Answer: Recording expenses helps track spending habits and improve money management.

Q: How does budgeting reduce financial stress?
Answer: Budgeting provides clarity on income and expenses, reducing uncertainty and stress.

LOAN & INTEREST BASICS

A loan is money borrowed from a bank or financial institution that must be repaid after a period, usually with additional money called interest.

Relatable Platforms

  • Bank education loans
  • Personal loan apps
  • EMI purchases

Why Loans Are Taken

  • Education
  • Buying a house
  • Starting a business
  • Medical needs
  • Purchasing vehicles

Types of Interest

Simple Interest

  • Interest is calculated only on the principal amount.
  • Formula: Simple Interest = (Principal × Rate × Time) ÷ 100
  • Simple Interest Calculator

Compound Interest

  • Interest is calculated on the principal and accumulated interest.
  • Formula: Amount = P(1 + R/100)ᵗ
  • Compound Interest Calculator

EMI

EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is a fixed amount paid every month to repay a loan, including both principal and interest.

Advantages of Loans

  • Helps meet big financial needs.
  • Enables asset ownership.
  • Builds credit history.

Disadvantages of Loans

  • Interest increases total cost.
  • Long-term financial commitment.
  • Missed payments affect credit score.

Important Q&A

Q: Is interest always bad?
A: No, but high interest loans can be risky.

Q: What happens if EMIs are not paid?
A: Penalties and poor credit score.

Q: Why is credit score important?
Answer: It affects loan approval and interest rate.

Q: What happens if EMIs are missed?
Answer: It affects credit score and may attract penalties.

BANKING BASICS

A bank is a safe place to keep money and manage financial transactions like deposits, withdrawals, gives loans when needed and transfers.

Banks act as a trusted place to manage money.

Types of Bank Accounts

  • Savings Account – For daily use, earns interest, used for salary, savings, and payments
  • Current Account – For businesses, no interest, higher transaction limits
  • Fixed Deposit (FD) – Money locked for fixed time, higher interest than savings

Important Banking Services

  • ATM withdrawals
  • Cheque payments
  • Online & mobile banking
  • Debit cards

Relatable Platforms

  • SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis Bank
  • Public & private sector banks

Important Q&A

Q: Is money safe in banks?
A: Yes, banks are regulated by RBI.

Q: Can students open a bank account?
A: Yes, student and zero-balance accounts are available.

Q: What is minimum balance?
A: It is the minimum amount required to keep in an account.

Q: Can I withdraw money anytime?
A: Yes, from savings accounts using ATM or bank branch.

Q: What happens if a bank closes?
Banks in India are regulated by RBI. Deposits are insured up to a certain limit, so customer money is protected.

TAX BASICS

Tax is money paid to the government to run public services like roads, education, and healthcare.

Types of Taxes

  • Direct Tax: Income tax paid on earnings
  • Indirect Tax: GST paid on goods and services

Who Should Pay Income Tax?

  • People earning above the basic exemption limit
  • Salaried employees, freelancers, business owners

Why Paying Tax is Important

  • Legal obligation
  • Helps national development
  • Avoids penalties and fines
  • To improve infrastructure
  • To help the government run the country

Relatable Platforms

  • Income Tax Portal (online filing)
  • GST (for businesses)

Simple Tax Safety Tips

  • Never share PAN details publicly
  • Use only official government websites
  • Keep income proofs safely
  • Avoid fake tax refund messages

Important Q&A

Q: Do students need to pay income tax?
A: Only if their income exceeds the exemption limit.

Q: Is filing tax return compulsory?
A: Filing is mandatory for eligible taxpayers.

Q: What is Tax Return?
A: A tax return is a form submitted to the government that shows your income, tax paid, and refund (if any). Filing returns is important even if tax is zero.

Q: What happens if tax is not paid?
A: Penalties and legal action may be taken.

Q: Can I pay tax online?
A: Yes, tax payments can be done online.

Q: What is tax refund?
A: Extra tax paid is returned by the government.

Q: What if I make a mistake while filing tax return?
A: It can be corrected later.
